Aortic Valve (TAVR/TAVI)

[NOTABLE] Michigan Medicine released comprehensive guidance on failed TAVR explantation procedures, detailing surgical options and associated risks for patients experiencing valve failure. This guidance comes as TAVR procedures approach their second decade, with early-generation devices now requiring consideration for surgical revision in selected cases. While TAVR failure rates remain low, the absolute number of patients requiring explantation continues to grow as procedure volumes increase. The Michigan team's approach emphasizes careful patient selection for explant procedures, noting that surgical mortality for TAVR explantation typically exceeds that of primary surgical aortic valve replacement due to patient comorbidities and technical complexities. This development highlights a critical gap in long-term TAVR management that will become increasingly relevant as younger patients receive transcatheter valves.

Regulatory & Policy

The Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services updated its tracking sheet for TAVR coverage under the National Coverage Analysis (NCA) framework. While specific policy changes weren't detailed in the update, this represents ongoing CMS oversight of TAVR reimbursement policies, which currently require participation in qualified clinical data registries and adherence to multidisciplinary heart teams. The tracking sheet system allows CMS to monitor coverage decisions and make adjustments based on emerging evidence and clinical experience.
