Today's Lead · Editorial

TAVR wins on mortality. Loses on durability.

The PARTNER 3 5-year extended follow-up published in NEJM this week shows a sustained mortality advantage for TAVR over SAVR in low-risk patients, but the structural valve deterioration signal in the TAVR arm at year 5 is the finding the device community is not talking about. Dr. Vinod Thourani's editorial calls for caution on extending the TAVR low-risk indication to patients under 65 until we see year 10 data. Edwards stock closed up 2.1% on the headline; the back-half of the paper is more sobering than the press release.

5 min read · By Michael Bowdish, MD
Mitral · EACTS

European surgeons say transcatheter mitral repair is not ready for prime time

EACTS publishes a position paper from a working group that includes Mehaffey and Chikwe. The conclusion: transcatheter mitral repair outcomes do not yet meet the threshold for routine use in surgical-eligible patients. The paper specifically calls out durability data and the absence of head-to-head trials against contemporary surgical repair.

Industry · Regulatory

FDA recalls 3,400 SAPIEN 3 Ultra valves over balloon catheter defect

Edwards Lifesciences disclosed the Class I recall in an 8-K this week. The defect affects a small lot shipped January through March 2026. No patient harm has been reported. The recall is a manufacturing issue, not a device performance signal, but the FDA classification is the highest tier.
